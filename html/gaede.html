F29 [Gaede]

Strab. 13, 1, 43: πολυπίδακον δὲ τὴν Ἴδην ἰδίως οἴονται λέγεσθαι διὰ τὸ πλῆθος τῶν ἐξ αὐτῆς ῥεόντων ποταμῶν καθ’ἃ μάλιστα ἡ Δαρδανικὴ ὑποπέπτωκεν αὐτῇ (καὶ) μέχρι Σκήψιος καὶ τὰ περὶ Ἴλιον. ἔμπειρος δ’ὢν τῶν τόπων ὡς ἂν ἐπιχώριος ἀνὴρ ὁ Δημήτριος τοτὲ μὲν οὕτως λέγει περὶ αὐτῶν

(Demetrios:) "ἔστι γὰρ λόφος τις τῆς Ἴδης Κότυλος· ὑπέρκειται δ’οὗτος ἑκατόν που καὶ εἴκοσι σταδίοις Σκήψιος ἐξ οὗ ὅ τε Σκάμανδρος ῥεῖ καὶ ὁ Γράνικος καὶ Αἴσηπος"

until § 45 C603 inspired by Demetrios

Apparatus

Translation

Strab. 13, 1, 43: It is believed that Mount Ida is pointedly called "many-fountained" because of the great number of rivers taking their spring from there, in particular in the region where the Dardanian plain lies beneath it until Scepsis and the territory of Ilion. Demetrios, being, as a native, well-acquainted with these places, spoke once in this way about them:

(Demetrios:) "there is indeed a hill of Mount Ida, called Kotylos; it lies about a hundred and twenty stadia above Scepsis and from it flow the Scamander, the Granicos and the Aesepus,"

F30 [Gaede]

a)

Eust. 890, 5: ἀπὸ τοῦ Καρήσου ποταμοῦ καὶ χώρα τις Καρησηνὴ λέγεται ἣν ὁ ἰδιωτισμὸς Κερασηνήν φησιν.

b)

Hesych. s. v. Κάρησσος: Κάρησος· πόλις καὶ ποταμός.

c)

Strab. 13, 1, 44: ἐμπίπτει (Hercher ex συμπίπτει corr.) δ᾿ εἰς αὐτὸν (scil. Σκάμανδρον) ὁ Ἄνδιρος ἀπὸ τῆς Καρησηνῆς, ὀρεινῆς τινὸς πολλαῖς κώμαις συνοικουμένης καὶ γεωργουμένης καλῶς,

Strab. 13, 1, 44: ὠνομάσθαι δὲ τὴν χώραν φασὶν ἀπὸ τοῦ Καρήσου ποταμοῦ

Strab. 13, 1, 44: τὴν δὲ πόλιν κατεσπάσθαι τὴν ὁμώνυμον τῷ ποταμῷ

Apparatus

Translation

a) Eust. 890, 5: From the river Karesos also a region takes Karesene as its name, to which the more common name of Kerasene refers.

b) Hesych. s. v. Κάρησσος: Karessos: a city and a river.

c) Strab. 13, 1, 44: And into the Scamander streams the Andiros, coming from the Caresene, a hilly region scattered with many villages and beautifully cultivated, (...)

Strab. 13, 1, 44: It is said that the region takes its name from the river Caresos (...)

Strab. 13, 1, 44: but that the city which had the same name as the river was destroyed.

Commentary

1) Gaede uses an edition of Hesychius where the toponym Karesos is spelt with one sigma. We give the spelling for the lemma as it appears in Latte's edition (Copenhagen 1953-1966).

2) Hercher's emendation from συμπίππτει to ἐμπίπτει is difficult to render in the translation.

3) In Gaede's text the name of the river Andiros is spelt Ἄνδιρος instead of Ἄνδειρος.

Apparatus

Translation

a) Schol. T Il. 12. 20a: (= Eust. 889, 59) Rhesos: it was renamed Rhoeites. It flows towards the north, down from the Beautiful Pine, which is 180 stadia away from Adramytteion.

b) Schol. T Il. 12. 20c: Karesos: it is called Pitys. It flows down from Malus. The inhabitants of Cyzicus pronounce it with an acute accent. It flows into the Aesepos.

c) Schol. T Il. 12. 20e: Rhodios: it is called Dardanos. It flows down from Kleandria, which is sixty stadia away from the Beautiful Pine.

Cf. Hesych. s.v. Ῥοδίος: Rhodios: a river. The present Dardanos.

Cf. The Rhodios flows down from Kleandria and Gordos, which are at 60 stadia away from the Beautiful Pine , and empties into the Ainion.
